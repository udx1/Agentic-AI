Section 1 Ś Terms of the Preferred Stock
1.1 Dividends
Holders of Series A Preferred Stock shall be entitled to receive non-cumulative dividends at a
rate of 8% per annum, payable only when and if declared by the Board of Directors.
No dividends shall be paid to holders of Common Stock unless and until all accrued dividends
on the Series A Preferred Stock have been paid in full.
1.2 Liquidation Preference
In the event of any liquidation, dissolution, winding-up, or deemed liquidation event, including a
merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, holders of Series A Preferred Stock shall
be entitled to receive, prior and in preference to any distribution to holders of Common Stock, an
amount equal to 1x the original purchase price per share, plus any declared but unpaid
dividends.
The liquidation preference is non-participating. After receipt of the full liquidation preference,
holders of Series A Preferred Stock shall have no further right or claim to the remaining assets
of the Company.
At the holderÆs option, Series A Preferred Stock may convert to Common Stock to participate in
any distribution that would yield a greater per-share return than the liquidation preference.
1.3 Conversion
Each share of Series A Preferred Stock shall be convertible at any time, at the option of the
holder, into one share of Common Stock, subject to adjustment for stock splits, dividends, and
recapitalizations.
All shares of Series A Preferred Stock shall automatically convert to Common Stock upon either
of the following:
1. The closing of a qualified IPO at a pre-money valuation of no less than $100,000,000; or
2. The written consent of holders of at least 60% of the outstanding Series A Preferred
Stock.
1.4 Anti-Dilution
Series A Preferred Stock shall be entitled to broad-based weighted average anti-dilution
protection in the event the Company issues new shares at a price below the Series A purchase
price, subject to customary exceptions, including shares issued pursuant to the employee option
pool, strategic partnerships, and equipment financings approved by the Board.
1.5 Voting Rights
Holders of Series A Preferred Stock shall vote on an as-converted basis together with the
holders of Common Stock on all matters submitted to a vote of stockholders.
Certain protective provisions shall require the separate approval of holders of at least 60% of
the outstanding Series A Preferred Stock, including:
1. Any amendment to the Certificate of Incorporation or Bylaws that adversely affects the
Series A;
2. Authorization or issuance of any equity security senior to or on parity with the Series A;
3. Any merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets;
4. Any increase or decrease in the authorized number of shares; and
5. Declaration of any dividend.

2.3 Board Meeting Cadence
The Board shall meet no less than quarterly, with monthly financial reporting provided to all
directors.
Section 3 Ś Investor Rights
3.1 Pro-Rata Rights
Apex Venture Partners shall have the right to participate in future equity financings on a pro-rata
basis, based on its fully diluted ownership percentage, to maintain its ownership stake through
and including the CompanyÆs Series B financing.
3.2 Information Rights
The Company shall deliver to Apex Venture Partners the following:
1. Unaudited monthly financial statements within 15 days of month-end;
2. Unaudited quarterly financials within 30 days of quarter-end;
3. Audited annual financials within 90 days of fiscal year-end; and
4. An annual operating budget approved by the Board prior to the start of each fiscal year.
3.3 Right of First Refusal
The Company shall grant Apex Venture Partners a right of first refusal on any proposed transfer
of shares by founders or other major stockholders, subject to customary exceptions.
3.4 Most Favored Nation
If the Company issues shares to any future investor on terms more favorable than those
contained herein, Apex Venture Partners shall have the right to elect those more favorable
terms with respect to its existing holdings.
Section 4 Ś Founder Terms
4.1 Vesting
All founder shares shall be subject to a 4-year vesting schedule with a 1-year cliff, with 25%
vesting on the one-year anniversary of each founderÆs start date and the remainder vesting
monthly thereafter.
Up to 25% of each founderÆs shares may be accelerated upon a change of control event
through single-trigger acceleration, with full acceleration available upon termination without
cause following a change of control through double-trigger acceleration.
4.2 Founder Lock-Up
Founders shall be subject to a 180-day lock-up period following any qualified IPO, during
which they may not sell, transfer, or otherwise dispose of Company shares without the prior
written consent of the lead underwriter.
Section 5 Ś Conditions to Closing
Closing of the financing shall be subject to the following conditions:
1. Completion of investor due diligence to Apex Venture PartnersÆ reasonable satisfaction;
2. Execution of definitive agreements, including a Stock Purchase Agreement, InvestorsÆ
Rights Agreement, Right of First Refusal and Co-Sale Agreement, and Voting
Agreement;
3. No material adverse change in the CompanyÆs business, financial condition, or
prospects; and
4. Customary legal opinions from Company counsel.
Section 6 Ś General Provisions
6.1 Exclusivity Ś Binding
For a period of 30 days from the date of execution of this Term Sheet, the Company agrees not
to solicit, initiate, or encourage any discussions with any other potential investor regarding a
financing on substantially similar terms.
This Section 6.1 is legally binding.
6.2 Expenses
The Company shall reimburse Apex Venture Partners for reasonable legal fees incurred in
connection with this transaction, not to exceed $25,000, payable at closing.
6.3 Confidentiality Ś Binding
The terms and existence of this Term Sheet shall be kept confidential by both parties and shall
not be disclosed to any third party without the prior written consent of the other party, except as
required by law.
This Section 6.3 is legally binding.
6.4 Non-Binding Nature
Except for Section 6.1 Ś Exclusivity, Section 6.2 Ś Expenses, and Section 6.3 Ś
Confidentiality, this Term Sheet is not a binding agreement and does not obligate either party
to consummate the proposed transaction.
A binding obligation will arise only upon execution of definitive transaction documents.
